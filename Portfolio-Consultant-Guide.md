## 🏆 4. Prompt Engineering Best Practices
Keep these core rules in mind for your next AI role:
1. **Context/Persona Assignment:** Never start a prompt cold. Always tell the AI *who* it is (e.g., "Act as a Senior Python Engineer") to calibrate its vocabulary and logic.
2. **Provide the Framework:** Don't ask the AI to "write an ad." Ask it to follow the "Hook, Agitate, Solve, CTA" framework.
3. **Show, Don't Tell (Zero-Shot vs. Few-Shot):** If a task is complex, provide one or two examples of a perfect input/output pair inside the prompt to anchor the model's expectations.
4. **Use Delimiters:** When passing large blocks of text (like transcripts or code), wrap them in triple quotes `"""` or XML tags `<data>` so the AI doesn't confuse the input data with your instructions.

## ❌ 5. Common Prompt Mistakes to Avoid
* **The "Kitchen Sink" Prompt:** Asking the AI to do 10 unrelated things in a single query. Break complex tasks into a chain of smaller prompts (Prompt Chaining).
* **Vague Adjectives:** Asking the AI to make a text "better" or "more professional." You must define what "better" means (e.g., "Use active voice, remove adverbs, and adhere to a 10th-grade reading level").
* **Ignoring Negative Constraints:** Forgetting to tell the AI what it *cannot* do, which often leads to hallucinations or formatting errors.
